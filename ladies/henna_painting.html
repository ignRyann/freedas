Background History

Henna has been used for hundreds of years as a natural plant dye. When applied to the skin, it produces a temporary stain similar to a tattoo but not permanent. Henna is completely natural, safe, and painless. It can be painted almost anywhere on the body, traditionally placed on the hands, feet, ankle, shins, and wrists where the skin is thickest to achieve a good dark stain. The henna paste hardens as it dries, and a mixture of lemon juice and sugar is applied to help darken the stain and keep the paste on for longer.
