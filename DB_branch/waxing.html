Background History

Waxing is a form of semi-permanent hair removal that removes hair from the root. It can be done on various body parts, including eyebrows, face, pubic area, legs, arms, back, abdomen, and feet. There are two main types of waxing: strip waxing and strip-less waxing. Strip waxing involves applying a thin layer of wax and removing it with a strip, while strip-less waxing involves applying and removing the wax without a strip. Strip-less waxing is often preferred for sensitive skin. Waxing provides long-lasting results, with hair regrowth typically occurring after four to six weeks.
