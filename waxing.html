Background History

Waxing is a form of semi-permanent hair removal that removes hair from the root. New hair will not grow back in the previously waxed area for four to six weeks. Almost any area of the body can be waxed, including eyebrows, face, pubic area (called bikini waxing), legs, arms, back, abdomen, and feet. There are many types of waxing suitable for removing unwanted hair.

Strip waxing (soft wax) involves spreading a thin layer of wax over the skin, applying a cloth or paper strip, and then quickly ripping it against the direction of hair growth. Strip-less waxing (hard wax) is applied thickly and removed without strips after hardening. This method is beneficial for sensitive skin as it adheres less to the skin and more to the hair, making it less painful.
